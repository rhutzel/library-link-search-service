Program & Outreach Coordinator ( Part-time) April 9, 2019

The Pennsauken Free Public Library is seeking a dynamic, creative individual for our new position of Program & Outreach Coordinator. This person will work collaboratively and individually to market and promote the Library's programs, collections, activities and mission, and to reach out to the community to bring in new patrons and create beneficial partnerships with other groups and agencies in Pennsauken and surrounding area.

Duties include (but not limited to):

  • Develop and oversee a coordinated and cohesive marketing strategy
  • Coordinate social media and public relations efforts
  • Write and distribute the Library newsletter and email announcements
  • Assist in the design of a cohesive style guide for marketing materials
  • Assist in grant-writing
  • Design posters, fliers, handouts, mailings and more
  • Oversee the in-library digital display
  • Write press releases and promotional items
  • Contribute to the library website
  • Be part of the Adult and Youth Services programming planning team
  • Identify potential community partnerships
  • Analyze program attendance and share findings
  • Conduct polls, surveys and questionnaires
  • Conduct outreach events
  • Help identify non-users

Qualifications:

4 year degree preferred.

Minimum years experience in marketing, communications or related field and/or in a library setting.

Knowledge of, and aptitude for, utilizing computers with Microsoft Office products and other technologies.

Superior oral and written communication skills.

Ability to work flexible hours, including days, nights and weekends.

The ability to work independently and be self-directed.

Manage detail, set priorities, meet deadlines, work quickly and accurately, follow directions, and maintain confidentiality.

Ability to manage multiple projects and work assignments in a fast-paced environment.

Knowledge of media relations, social networking tools and marketing.

High level of interpersonal and organizational skills necessary for working with a number of constituents including library staff, trustees, volunteers, independent contractors vendors, community and corporate groups, and the general public.

Must possess a valid Motor Vehicle Operators License and means of transportation to fulfill job duties in New Jersey and surrounding areas.

DESIRABLE EXPERIENCE:

Bachelor's degree in marketing, communications or related subject required; MLS or MLS candidate a bonus.

Excellent skills in graphic design; and experience with Canva, Adobe Photoshop Elements, Constant Contact and any other graphic design tool.

Speak fluent Spanish and/or Vietnamese

Salary Range:$21-$25
Hours:25 hours per week, including some evenings and weekends as required

To Apply:Submit cover letter, and resume in PDF format to Library Director, Tanya Finney Estrada via email atdirector@pennsaukenlibrary.org

Make sure you add Program & Outreach Coordinator to the subject line.

Open until filled but applications received by June 1st will be given priority.

Pennsauken Township residents given preference.

For more information you can call (856)665-5959
Library Associate F/T Marketing position April 8, 2019

Warren County Library has an exciting opportunity for a full time (35 hours per week) Marketing Manager.

Warren County Library serves a population of 75,000 people through 4 library branches and bookmobile.

Our mission is to meet the changing needs of our communities by providing information and environments that promote personal growth and community transformation. The Marketing Manager plays a crucial role in meeting these needs and serving the patrons of WCL.

The Marketing Manager will develop, strategize and implement improvements to communication between staff, departments, public, media, and other outside agencies. The Manager will develop communications via print and electronic media including social media and a monthly Newsletter/eNewsletter. This position works with WCL administration and management team to develop marketing and promotions such as signage, print publications, electronic content, press and media announcements, and social media.

Marketing and Promotions Initiatives

The Marketing Manager will attend department meetings and sub-committees that are working on developing programming, improving user experience, and website. They will lead a committee that will establish a brand and graphic standards; oversee social media messaging and the creation and development of promotional materials for library programs and events. The Marketing Manager produces and disseminates public information via print, video and electronic resources. The Manager evaluates the success of communication endeavors through services such as Google Analytics, Google Business, SEO optimization, and statistical and analytical analysis.

Job requirements

BA in Communications Studies or similar course of studies

Course work in graphic design, writing, social media, marketing, film work.

Salary

$35,923

Resumes: Maureen Baker Wilkinson, Director, Warren County Library, 2 Shotwell Drive, Belvidere, NJ 07823 mbwilkinson@warrenlib.org
Part Time Library Assistant-Clark, NJ April 8, 2019

The Clark Public Library is hiring a part-time LIBRARY ASSISTANT. This position will be part-time with a flexible schedule that may include days, evenings and some weekends. Applicant must be available to work various shifts, including evenings and the occasional weekend. Hours total 20 per week. Must be available to work at least a 4 hour shift between the hours of 9 a.m. and 9 p.m. Monday through Friday. Hours will vary when weekend shift occurs. Employees are entitled to sick leave and vacation time. Starting salary for position is $15.21 per hour. This position is Civil Service/Union.

General Summary:

The Library Assistant performs general reception duties including fielding in-person and on the telephone informational and directional questions from patrons.

Must be able to exercise judgment in public service situations and perform several jobs simultaneously.

Performs procedures related to circulation of materials, including check in and out of library material, collection of fees and fines and registration of library members.

Processes new library material and performs basic repairs to damaged library material.

Works effectively and courteously with the general public and other employees.

Education, Experience, and Training:

High School diploma or equivalent required.

Computer proficiency in Microsoft Windows and Office products required.

Keyboarding skill required.

Phone and office work experience required.

Customer service experience in a library, office or retail environment is required.

CPR training a plus.

Deadline for Applications: April 30, 2019

HOW TO APPLY: Please respond with a resume and the names of three references via email to circulation@clarklibrary.org by Tuesday, April 30, 2019. NO phone calls, no faxed resumes, no mailed resumes. Due to the high number of responses, we will only respond to candidates who have been selected for interviews.

FT Adult Services Librarian II - Emerging Technologies/Digital - Hunterdon County Library April 1, 2019

Primary t

The Hunterdon County Library (HCL) is seeking an energetic team-oriented candidate with a strong public service commitment to provide leadership in the organization in the area of emerging technologies and digital librarianship in Hunterdon County. This position reports to the Head of Adult Services.

The Hunterdon County Library (HCL) is seeking an energetic team-oriented candidate with a strong public service commitment to provide leadership in the organization in the area of emerging technologies and digital librarianship in Hunterdon County. This position reports to the Head of Adult Services.

The Hunterdon County Library System consists of two regional libraries, one small but active satellite library, a bookmobile and ongoing support to seven affiliated libraries within the County of Hunterdon. The current staff consists of over 70 full and part-time employees. Details about the Hunterdon Library can be found at: http://www.hclibrary.us/

RESPONSIBILITIES (MAY INCLUDE BUT NOT LIMITED TO):

  • Provides advanced reference and readers advisory services; providing backup reference services in the youth services department as needed.
  • Provides leadership, including statistical reports, in providing access to online collections and resources including the library website, databases, and social media.
  • Plans creative, engaging, and age-appropriate programs and/or technical instruction for emerging technologies and digital librarianship; incorporates appropriate adult learning theories; develops programs.
  • Trains staff and public to use print and online library resources, electronic resources, eBooks and readers, emerging technologies and provides digital literacy training and resources
  • Collaborates with other organizations in the county, state, and countrywide to best serve the residents of Hunterdon County.
  • Presents programs independently or in collaboration with community organizations for a range of community interests.
  • May take the lead over Librarians 1 and/or paraprofessional/technical staff, coordinate assignments, and provide instructions for their completion.
  • Coordinates with the Technical Services Department to develop assigned area of collection making informed purchase recommendations based upon established criteria that anticipate and meet public demand including all digital media.
  • Deselects materials on an ongoing and consistent basis according to established criteria and procedures and as local needs dictate.
  • Takes the initiative by identifying, evaluating, and assisting in implementing emerging technologies.
  • Integrates technological solutions for everyday challenges and streamlines procedures.
  • Maintains the integrity of the collection by evaluating materials based upon established criteria.
  • Plans, promotes, and implements outreach services to organizations which may include schools, local businesses, and other outside agencies and community organizations to promote library services and build community capital including on-site visits, presenting programs, or special events.
  • Attends seminars, meetings, and conferences of professional and technical organizations for the purpose of keeping informed about current and emerging library services and technical developments.
  • Working on special projects as assigned by supervisor and/or Library Administration.

REQUIREMENTS:

  • ALA Accredited Masters degree in Library or Information Sciences and possess or be able to obtain a New Jersey Professional Library Certificate.
  • Minimum of one years professional librarian experience providing reference and readers advisory services and programming for the public.

PREFERRED SKILLS:

  • Knowledge of print and non-print reference and readers advisory resources, as well as changes in technology and trends within the library profession.
  • Excellent computer and technological skills, including but not limited to proficiency with social media, downloadable media and various technological devices.
  • Ability to translate new and emerging technologies into services that align with the librarys mission and community needs.
  • Ability to effectively use and demonstrate technology tools including the web, email, electronic databases, e-readers, office applications, as well as other library related programs; ability to learn and adapt to new technologies as required.
  • Interpersonal skills ability to work and interact effectively with others in a positive manner, as well as work under pressure and/or manage stressful situations with courtesy and tact.
  • Customer service skills ability to provide exceptional service to internal and external customers; exceptional oral and written English communications skills; ability to manage multiple projects and meet deadlines.
  • Ability to respond to or handle emergency situations effectively as the in-charge person or in cooperation with the in-charge person.
  • Applicant must comply with the NJ First Act.
  • Appointee may be required to possess a valid driver's license - initial appointment provisional pending Civil Service procedure; 35 hours/week, including nights & weekends.

Librarian 2 title requires a MLS from an ALA or NJ College Masters program in Library Science that has be deemed acceptable by the Thomas Edison State College.

The Librarian 2 title is a Civil Service governed job position and additional information can be found at https://info.csc.state.nj.us/jobspec/74083.htm

SCHEDULE

  • Tuesday 1:30pm to 9:00pm
  • Wednesday 9:00am to 5:00pm
  • Thursday 9:00am to 5:00pm
  • Friday 9:00am to 5:00pm
  • Saturday 9:00am to 5:00pm
Job Application Deadline: Monday, April 15, 2019

Hunterdon County Library offers pension, medical, and prescription benefits, voluntary vision and dental plans, self-funded deferred compensation, paid sick, vacation, personal and holiday leave.
How To Apply::

Interested applicants should mail a cover letter and resume to Human Resources at 71 Main Street, PO Box 2900, Flemington, NJ 08822 or by email at personnel@co.hunterdon.nj.us
FT Adult Services Librarian- Cherry Hill Public Library April 1, 2019

Adult Services Librarian

The Cherry Hill Public Library is seeking to hire a creative, well-read librarian for the Reference & Adult Services Department. We are a state of the art library in a thriving and diverse suburban community. The successful candidate will be a responsible and energetic individual ready to keep up within a busy department.

Research, initiate, and maintain outreach relationships to Cherry Hill community organizations, businesses, and demographics (such as immigrant and residents with disabilities) along with the Supervisor of Diversity & Outreach.

Initiate and participate in departmental programming and services such as (but not limited to) the Reference by Appointment service, our Coffee and Conversation program series, monthly Book Club, and other stand-alone events.

Research, identify, apply, and manage grants that create or support library mission materials and services.

Selecting adult fiction and/or non-fiction material as part of the collection development group Staffing the adult reference desk as scheduled

Teaching computer and technology classes to the general public as part of a rotation

Identify and promote library services to special populations, including people with disabilities, new immigrants, and older adults.

Qualifications:

Must have a masters degree in Library or Information Science from an American Library Association accredited college or university

Experience providing reference and readers advisory services

Strong communication and customer service skills

Knowledge of print and digital general knowledge resources

Ability to work both independently and within a team

Teaching or computer instruction experience a plus

Knowledge of Koha ILS a plus

Successfully pass a criminal background check

New Jersey residency required pursuant to NJSA 54:14-7 (L. 2011, Chapter 70), if selected for the position

Schedule: This is a full time (35 hours a week) position with one weeknight and rotating Saturdays and Sundays required.

About the Library: The Cherry Hill Public Library is the largest municipal library in the southern New Jersey area. The Library is open seven days a week and is operation Monday through Thursday from 9:30 am to 9:00 pm, Friday and Saturday 9:30 am to 5:00 pm, and 1:00 pm to 5:00 pm 32 Sundays a year.

How to Apply: All employment applications (cover letter and resume) may be emailed to jpurcell@chplnj.org with the subject line Adult Services Librarian or mailed to the address below: Administration Cherry Hill Public Library 1100 Kings Highway North Cherry Hill, NJ 08034 Attention: Jennie Purcell -Adult Services Librarian
